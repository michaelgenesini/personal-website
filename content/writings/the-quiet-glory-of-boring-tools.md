There’s something suspicious about every “next big thing.”
You can almost hear the marketing department whispering behind it.

Meanwhile, the tools that quietly **work**—the ones that never make headlines—just keep doing their job year after year.
They don’t have fancy dashboards or AI-powered assistants.
They just _function_, like a well-oiled wrench.

---

### The Boredom Premium

You can tell a mature engineer by what excites them.
When you stop chasing novelty for its own sake, you start appreciating stability.
A migration that doesn’t break production? Bliss.
A dependency that hasn’t changed since 2018? _Chef’s kiss._

> Reliability is the most underrated form of innovation.

It’s not about avoiding change—it’s about earning the right to move slowly.
There’s nothing heroic about rewriting something just because you’re bored.

---

### A Short List of Boring Greatness

1. `cron`
2. `rsync`
3. Markdown itself (the irony, I know)
4. SQLite
5. Anything written in plain text that will still open in 2040

These aren’t glamorous. They don’t trend.
But they survive _every_ hype cycle like cockroaches of sanity.

---

### The Paradox

Everyone wants a tool that feels _simple_, but nobody wants to build one.
Simplicity is punishingly hard to maintain.
Every feature request chips away at it until one day, you’ve built the very thing you swore you’d replace.

```ts
function complexityCreep(project) {
  while (true) {
    project.addFeature()
    project.addDependency()
    if (project.stability < 0) break
  }
}
```
